CONTEXT SETTING

How Our O&M Engagements Are Structured

Hospital operations cannot be standardised into a fixed pricing format.

Each hospital differs in:

  • Bed strength and infrastructure
  • Specialty mix and clinical complexity
  • Ownership structure (private, trust, institutional)
  • Staffing patterns and leadership depth
  • Operational maturity and existing systems
  • Level of control and involvement required

For this reason, our O&M engagements are structured based on actual operational needs, not predefined packages.

👉 The objective is not just to manage but to create a system that performs consistently
COMMERCIAL STRUCTURES

Typical Commercial Models

Revenue-Linked O&M Model

Overview:
In this model, our engagement is aligned with the hospital’s operational performance. Pricing is structured as a percentage of gross turnover, ensuring that our role remains closely connected to the hospital’s overall functioning and growth.

Typical Range: 3% to 5% of gross turnover

This depends on:

  • Hospital size and bed capacity
  • Number of departments under management
  • Level of operational responsibility
  • Extent of onsite involvement
  • Complexity of services and workflows

Best suited for:

  • Full operations takeover
  • Large or multi-specialty hospitals
  • Long-term engagements
  • Institutions seeking aligned performance incentives
👉 This model creates alignment between operational discipline and financial outcomes

Fixed Monthly Retainer Model

Overview:
For hospitals requiring structured support without full operational takeover, pricing is offered as a fixed monthly engagement. This model is commonly used for hybrid, remote, or department-specific O&M structures.

Typical Range: ₹1,00,000 to ₹5,00,000 per month

This depends on:

  • Engagement model selected
  • Number of physical visit days
  • Scope of dashboard and reporting systems
  • Level of remote monitoring
  • Management and implementation depth

Best suited for:

  • Hybrid / remote O&M
  • Phased engagement models
  • Smaller or mid-sized hospitals
  • Institutions starting structured O&M
👉 This model offers flexibility while maintaining professional oversight
ENGAGEMENT APPROACH

How Hospitals Typically Begin

Most hospitals do not immediately move into full O&M. A structured approach is usually more effective:

01

Phase 1: Stabilization & Control

Hybrid or department-specific engagement (focus on visibility, control, and immediate corrections)

02

Phase 2: System Integration

Stronger involvement with deeper system integration (improved coordination, reporting, and accountability)

03

Phase 3: Operational Alignment

Full operational alignment if required (complete system-driven functioning)

👉 This phased approach ensures minimal disruption and stronger long-term outcomes
WHAT YOUR INVESTMENT COVERS

VALUE PERSPECTIVE

How O&M Impacts Overall Performance

In most hospitals, operational inefficiencies are not immediately visible. They rarely appear as direct losses on financial statements. Instead, they exist silently within daily operations, gradually affecting performance, margins, patient experience, and long-term sustainability.

They typically appear in the form of:

Billing gaps and missed charge capture
Delayed discharges affecting bed availability
TPA inefficiencies and claim delays
Poor interdepartmental coordination
Underutilised infrastructure and resources
Inconsistent processes across departments

Deeper hidden inefficiencies:

Consultant engagement and alignment
Weak or unstructured marketing efforts
Lack of consistent hospital branding
Suboptimal case mix affecting profitability
Staff attitude and patient handling behaviour
Absence of experienced operational leadership
Unclear roles and accountability structures
Overdependence on individuals instead of systems
Poor OPD to IPD conversion tracking
Ineffective scheduling of OTs and procedures
Lack of real-time dashboards and decision visibility
Weak follow-up systems and patient retention
Gaps in documentation impacting billing
Vendor mismanagement and cost leakages
Absence of standard operating protocols
Unstructured pricing and inconsistencies
Inadequate training and skill development
Lack of ownership at department levels
Inconsistent patient experience across touchpoints

👉 Individually, these may seem small. But collectively, they create a significant impact on:

Revenue realisation Operational efficiency Patient satisfaction Staff productivity Overall hospital performance

👉 Structured O&M identifies, aligns, and corrects these gaps, bringing visibility, control, and predictability into the system.
